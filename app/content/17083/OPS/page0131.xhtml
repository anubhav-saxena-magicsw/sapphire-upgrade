Diffusion is the movement of a substance from an area of higher concentration to an area of lower concentration. The diffusion of a liquid, such as water, across a membrane is called osmosis. Some substances diffuse freely across cell membranes, while others must enter through protein channels in a process called facilitated diffusion. Some substances are trans-ported into the cell by transport proteins against, or up, a concentration gradient. This process requires the cell to expend energy and is called active transport.
