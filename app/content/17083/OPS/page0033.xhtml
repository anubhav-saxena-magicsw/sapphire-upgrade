There is sometimes a link between the environment and disease, as is the case when polluted air or water or the presence of disease-carrying organisms makes people sick. With such social, economic, and environmental relationships disease is a major factor in the sustainability of populations and communities. Sustainability is the ability to meet a community’s present needs without compromising the ability of future genera tions to meet their own needs.In this unit, you will study life at the cellular level and see how research by cell biologists and medical professionals has advanced society’s efforts to cure and prevent diseases. In this activity you will examine health indicators as you look at world health from a sustainability perspective.
