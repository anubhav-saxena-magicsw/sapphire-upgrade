Most horsetails live in wet sandy soil; some are semi-aquatic and some live in wet clay soil. Examples: Equisetum, CalamitaceaePhylum Cycadophyta (cycads)Cycads are vascular, tropical and subtropical, evergreen seed plants with large compound leaves. eir cones are either male pollen-producing or female seed-producing.Phylum Ginkgophyta (ginkgoes)Ginkgoes, vascular woody trees, have fan-shaped leaves. ere are male pollen-producing trees, and female seed-producing trees, which have ﬂeshy ovules. Only one species (Ginkgo biloba) exists.Phylum Gnetophyta (gnetophytes)e gnetophytes are woody vascular plants that live mostly in desert environments with some elements of ﬂowering plants. Example: WelwitschiaPhylum Coniferophyta (conifers)Most of the conifers, woody vascular trees with seed-bearing cones, are evergreen and have needle-like leaves. Examples: juniper, blue spruce, hemlock, white pinePhylum Anthophyta (flowering plants)e ﬂowering plants are vascular and seed-producing plants. ey have fruits that enclose the seeds, and an endosperm within the seeds.Examples: iris, lily, corn, dandelion, wild rosetree fernhorsetailginkgodandelion
