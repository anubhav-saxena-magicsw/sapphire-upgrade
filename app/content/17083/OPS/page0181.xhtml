Adults exchange gas through their skin or lungs, but larvae have gills for gas exchange. Adults of most species live on land, but lay their eggs in water where the young develop.Examples: salamanders, frogs and toads, caeciliansCLASS REPTILIA (REPTILES)Reptiles have a bony skeleton, lungs, and dry and scaly skin. Most have two pairs of limbs, three-chambered hearts, and are ectothermic (cold-blooded). Females’ eggs are fertilized internally, and they lay eggs with leathery shells.
