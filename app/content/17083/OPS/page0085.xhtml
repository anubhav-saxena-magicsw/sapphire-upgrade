To transport materials into the cell, the membrane forms a pocket that then pinches into the cell, forming a vesicle. This formation and movement of vesicles into the cell is called endocytosis. To transport materials out of the cell, a vesicle in the cytoplasm fuses with the cell membrane, and re-forms a smooth outer membrane, releasing its contents to the outside.
