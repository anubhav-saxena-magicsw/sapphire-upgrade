NDERSTANDING THE CELL membrane is the key to understanding many dis-eases, and is of great value in developing treatments. Some diseases dis-able the ability of the cell membrane to reseal after it has been penetrated, and some destroy the function of the cell’s transport channels. The immune system recognizes foreign cells through receptors on the membrane of both the foreign and immune cells. Many disease-causing pathogens, such as the HIV/AIDS virus and the polio virus, infect humans by recognizing and binding to the cell mem-brane, and then entering the cell.
