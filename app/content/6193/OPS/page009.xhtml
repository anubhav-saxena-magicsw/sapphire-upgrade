Instantaneous Velocity Instantaneous velocity is the velocity at one instant, while average velocity is averaged over a longer period of time. Instantaneous velocity is determined by measuring velocity at a certain points of the race which can be used to calculate maximum speed and when runners are accelerating or decelerating.
