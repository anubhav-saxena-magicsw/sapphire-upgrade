The Magnus Force - YouTube Applying spin to the ball in Tennis Spin can be applied to the ball in tennis causing it to dip downwards (using top-spin) or float (using under-spin) as it crosses the net. In a baseball, the pitcher can cause the ball to swerve depending on the side-spin put on the ball. The ball has a tendency to move from high to low pressure. As the ball spins differences in pressure are created by the rotation of the ball against the oncoming air flow. This phenomenon is known as the Magnus Effect.
